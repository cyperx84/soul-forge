## Who are you, and how would you introduce yourself to someone you respect?
> Your name and what you actually do — in your own words. Skip the job title if a title doesn't capture it.

## What's the path that got you here — and what did it teach you about how you work?
> The jobs, projects, detours, or hard lessons that shaped how you think. Not a résumé; the formative bits.

## What are you actually trying to accomplish right now?
> Your 1-3 real goals. What would make this season of work feel like a win? Be concrete.

## Where are you the expert in the room — and what's a strong opinion you hold that others in your field push back on?
> Where people come to you for advice, and where you're certain while others hedge. Convictions are what make an agent sharp instead of generic.

## What are you deliberately trying to get better at?
> Skills, ideas, or habits you're investing in. What's on your backlog, and what's the gap you're trying to close?

## Show me how you sound — paste or describe something you wrote that sounds exactly like you.
> A message, comment, or note in your real voice. Then: what words or moves would feel fake coming from an agent imitating you?

## Think of the last time someone you worked with genuinely annoyed you. What did they do?
> The communication habits, work patterns, or attitudes that grate on you. A real moment beats a generic pet peeve — and tells the agent what to never become.

## Tell me about the last time you had to act before you had enough information. What did you actually do?
> Walk through the real moment, not the theory. Did you push for clarity first, or start and adjust? A specific story shows how you move when the picture is incomplete.

## When and how do you actually work?
> Your time zone and the hours you're typically at it. Focused deep-work blocks, or always-on and reactive? When are you sharpest?

## Walk me through a day where you did your best work. What made it click?
> Energy, focus, the kind of problems, who you talked to. What conditions bring out your best — and what an agent could do to protect them?

## What would the person who knows your work best say is the most frustrating thing about collaborating with you?
> The blind spot you might not volunteer on your own. Be honest — naming it tells an agent where to give you room, and where to push back.

## What earns your trust in someone's work — and what instantly loses it?
> The signals that tell you someone is sharp and reliable, and the tells that make you stop trusting their output.
