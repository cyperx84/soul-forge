## What is your name, and what do you do?
> Your role, title, or how you'd introduce yourself professionally. Don't overthink it.

## What's your professional background?
> How did you get here? Key jobs, domains, or pivots that shaped how you think and work.

## What are you currently trying to accomplish?
> Your 1-3 main goals right now — what would make this quarter or year feel like a win?

## What areas do you consider yourself an expert in?
> Be honest about both breadth and depth. Where do people come to you for advice?

## What are you actively trying to learn or improve?
> Skills, domains, or habits you're investing in. What's on your reading list or learning backlog?

## How do you prefer to communicate?
> Direct and terse? Verbose with context? Do you prefer bullet points or prose? Examples help.

## What time zone and working hours do you keep?
> When are you typically at your keyboard? Do you work in focused blocks or stay-on all day?

## What frustrates you in professional interactions?
> Things that slow you down, communication anti-patterns, or work habits that drive you up the wall.

## How do you handle uncertainty or ambiguity?
> Do you push for clarity up front, or prefer to start and adjust? How do you make decisions without full information?

## What does a great working day look like for you?
> Walk through your ideal day — energy, focus, output, interactions.
